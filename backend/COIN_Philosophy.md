COIN Philosophy

Contextual Observation Intelligence Network

Version 1.0

⸻

Purpose

COIN exists to answer a single question:

What is the most important bullpen story happening right now, and why?

BaseballOS is not a prediction engine.

It is not a betting model.

It is not a collection of metrics.

COIN exists to transform baseball events into trustworthy baseball intelligence.

Its responsibility is to discover meaningful bullpen stories, explain why they matter, and expose the evidence that supports them.

⸻

The Philosophy

BaseballOS should never ask someone to trust a conclusion without exposing the reasoning behind it.

Every conclusion should be explainable.

Every explanation should be supported.

Every supporting statement should ultimately trace back to factual baseball data.

The product should always answer three questions:

What happened?

Why does it matter?

How do we know?

⸻

The Trust Hierarchy

Every BaseballOS narrative follows the same chain.

Story
↓
Observations
↓
Evidence
↓
Facts

Story

The narrative.

The answer to:

“What is the bullpen story?”

Example:

“The Giants couldn’t protect the game shape they created.”

Stories are written for humans.

Stories should be readable.

Stories should never invent facts.

⸻

Observations

Observations explain why BaseballOS reached the story.

They are deterministic.

They are not opinions.

Examples:

* The bullpen entered with a lead.
* The bullpen lost the lead.
* Late runs accumulated.
* The starter reduced bullpen burden.

Observations explain the reasoning.

⸻

Evidence

Evidence proves the observations.

Evidence is structured.

Evidence contains no prose.

Examples:

* Largest lead: 4
* Late runs: 7
* Starter: Landen Roupp — 6.0 IP
* Ryan Walker — 4 ER

Evidence is never summarized.

It simply exposes the facts.

⸻

Facts

Facts originate from MLB data.

Facts should never be invented.

Examples:

* innings pitched
* pitches thrown
* score
* bullpen entry score
* reliever appearances
* player names
* workload
* availability

Facts are BaseballOS’s foundation.

⸻

COIN Principles

1. Truth over creativity.

If BaseballOS cannot prove something, it should not say it.

Unknown is preferable to incorrect.

⸻

2. Fail closed.

Missing information should reduce confidence.

It should never produce fabricated certainty.

⸻

3. Deterministic intelligence.

The same inputs should always produce the same outputs.

Randomness has no place inside COIN.

⸻

4. Intelligence before presentation.

COIN discovers baseball intelligence.

Presentation layers communicate it.

Presentation must never create intelligence.

⸻

5. Every layer has one responsibility.

COIN is intentionally layered.

Each layer answers one question.

⸻

COIN Architecture

MLB Data
↓
Completed Game Context
"What happened?"
↓
Narrative Context
"Why did it matter?"
↓
Narrative Feed
"What should be communicated?"
↓
Evidence Composition Engine
"What evidence supports this?"
↓
Story Package
"Should this be published?"
↓
Story Writers
"How should humans read this?"
↓
Presentation

Every layer has exactly one responsibility.

No layer should duplicate another.

⸻

Layer Responsibilities

Completed Game Context

Transforms raw MLB data into structured game facts.

No narratives.

No opinions.

No prose.

⸻

Narrative Context

Determines the baseball story.

Examples:

* Lost Game Shape
* Protected Game Shape
* Bullpen Kept Team Alive
* Bullpen Overexposed

Narrative Context identifies the story.

It does not write it.

⸻

Narrative Feed

Packages everything required for storytelling.

No decisions are made downstream.

Story writers should never query baseball engines.

⸻

Evidence Composition Engine

Organizes facts into explainable evidence.

Evidence proves observations.

Evidence never contains prose.

⸻

Story Package

Determines:

* publishability
* priority
* destinations
* surfaces

The Story Package is the editorial contract.

⸻

Story Writers

Story Writers are translators.

They do not think.

They do not reason.

They do not query baseball engines.

They simply translate Story Packages into readable baseball language.

⸻

Editorial Philosophy

Stories tell the story.

Observations explain the story.

Evidence proves the observations.

Facts support the evidence.

Every BaseballOS story should be auditable.

⸻

What BaseballOS Is

BaseballOS is not trying to tell every baseball story.

It is trying to identify the bullpen story that matters most.

Sometimes that story is obvious.

Sometimes it is hidden inside the final score.

Sometimes it is hidden inside a reliever’s workload.

Sometimes it is hidden inside the game shape a team created but could not protect.

COIN exists to find those stories.

⸻

Product Philosophy

The frontend is not the product.

The dashboard is not the product.

The charts are not the product.

The stories are not the product.

The intelligence is the product.

Everything else exists to communicate it.

⸻

Future Expansion

COIN was designed around bullpen intelligence.

Its architecture intentionally supports future intelligence domains.

Examples include:

* Rotation Intelligence
* Injury Intelligence
* Schedule Intelligence
* Series Intelligence
* Prospect Intelligence
* Organizational Intelligence

New domains should plug into COIN’s architecture rather than replacing it.

⸻

Final Principle

Every time a BaseballOS user asks,

“Why does BaseballOS believe that?”

there should always be an answer.

Not because the system is persuasive.

Because the system is explainable.

That is the standard COIN is designed to uphold.
